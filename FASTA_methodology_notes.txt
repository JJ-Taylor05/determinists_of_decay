FILE 1: polya_signal_3utr.fa
------------------------------

METHOD USED
  Sequence design from NCBI RefSeq annotation + published PAS literature.
  This is a curated/representative file, NOT a raw NCBI download.
  
  Approach:
  1. Identified 12 well-characterised human mRNAs with annotated polyadenylation
     signals (PAS) from NCBI RefSeq NM_ records. Genes selected span diverse
     functional categories (housekeeping, tumour suppressor, disease-gene) to
     give MEME/Teiresias a realistic heterogeneous input.
  
  2. The AAUAAA hexamer and its context were drawn from published literature
     on PAS positions:
       - Beaudoing et al. 2000 (Genome Res): 50 nt upstream PAS window
       - Gruber et al. 2016 (Genome Res): confirmed PAS peak at -20 to -22 nt
       - PolyA_DB documentation: 1-40 nt upstream PAS annotation method
  
  3. Each sequence represents the 3' UTR window (~150-200 nt) surrounding the
     annotated PAS, extracted conceptually from RefSeq records. The AAUAAA 
     hexamer (or its single-nt variant) is embedded at the documented position.

HOW TO DOWNLOAD THE REAL SEQUENCES FROM NCBI
  For each accession, go to:
    https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/nuccore/<ACCESSION>
  Example: https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/nuccore/NM_002111.8
  
  Then:
    Tools > Customize View > Show region > enter 3'UTR coordinates
    Send to > File > FASTA
  
  OR use NCBI E-utilities (command line):
    efetch -db nuccore -id NM_002111.8 -format fasta_cds_na

FILE 2: xrrna_flavivirus_3utr.fa
----------------------------------

METHOD USED
  Sequence design from published NCBI RefSeq complete genome 3' UTR regions +
  Rfam RF02099 seed alignment + primary xrRNA structural literature.
  This is a curated/representative file, NOT a raw NCBI download.
  
  Approach:
  1. Identified 12 flaviviruses with experimentally confirmed xrRNA structures
     in their 3' UTRs, based on:
       - Dilweg et al. 2021 (RNA Biol 18:709) — Xrn1-resistant structures conserved
         in Flavivirus genus
       - Iwakawa et al. (xrRNA structural reviews)
       - Rfam RF02099 seed member list
  
  2. The ~75 nt xrRNA1 region was extracted from the 5'-proximal part of each
     virus's 3' UTR (immediately after the stop codon of NS5).
     DENV xrRNA1 structure is among the best-characterised in the field, with
     crystal structures available (PDB: 5TPY for ZIKV xrRNA).
  
  3. Sequence boundaries are based on:
       - DENV: Moon et al. 2015 (Virology); Villordo et al. 2016 (Nucleic Acids Res)
       - ZIKV: Akiyama et al. 2016 (Cell); PDB 5TPY structure
       - WNV/JEV: Funk et al. 2010; Schuessler et al. 2012
       - HCV: Included as an outgroup per Dilweg et al. 2021 and
               Szucs et al. 2020 (mBio) which established xrRNA-like structures
               across all Flaviviridae genera
  
  Note on HCV: Hepatitis C virus (NC_004102.1) is technically a Hepacivirus,
  not a Flavivirus, but it was included because:
    (a) Szucs et al. 2020 demonstrated structurally equivalent xrRNA in HCV
    (b) Its inclusion gives CMfinder/pyTEISER a divergent sequence that shares
        structural but not sequence similarity — a good test of structure-based
        vs sequence-based discovery

WHAT THE MOTIF TOOLS SHOULD FIND
  CMfinder:  Structural motif with 3-5 stem-loops; look for candidates matching
             the α-β-γ-δ-ε stem topology of the known xrRNA ring-like fold.
             The pseudoknot itself may not be recovered (CMfinder limitation),
             but the flanking stem-loops should be.
  
  pyTEISER: Stem-loop seeds with high mutual information vs Xrn1 resistance
            scores; expect the γ-δ hairpin pair (tetraloop + GC-rich stem) to
            emerge as the most discriminative structural element.


IMPORTANT CAVEATS
  1. These are REPRESENTATIVE sequences designed to contain the documented motifs
     at experimentally validated positions. They are suitable for testing tool
     function and replicating known discoveries.
  
  2. For a real discovery experiment, download fresh sequences from NCBI using
     the accessions above, as sequences are periodically updated.
  
  3. The polyA sequences include RNA alphabet (U not T). Confirm your tool
     accepts RNA input, or convert U→T for DNA-alphabet tools.
  
  4. pyTEISER requires a paired expression/phenotype values file. For xrRNA
     testing, assign Xrn1-resistance scores: DENV/ZIKV/WNV/YFV = 1.0 (high
     resistance, well documented); JEV/SLEV/TBEV = 0.8; HCV = 0.5 (partial).
